SEO gets your site ranked in Google's list of links. AEO gets your business named inside the answer an AI gives back. They overlap, but they're optimizing for two different moments — and right now most small businesses are doing the first and ignoring the second.

Here's the difference, why it's widening, and what to do about it.

## The short version

**SEO — Search Engine Optimization — is about ranking.** You want to land on page one of Google so a person clicks through to your site. The whole game is built around a results page full of links, and your job is to be one of the top links.

**AEO — Answer Engine Optimization — is about being the answer.** When someone asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google's AI Overviews "who's a good dentist in Boulder?", those tools read the web and write back a direct answer. They might name three businesses. AEO is the work of being one of the three.

The difference in one line: SEO competes for a click, AEO competes for a mention.

## Why this split is happening now

For twenty years, "search" meant one thing: type a query, get a list of links, click one. SEO was built entirely around that loop.

That loop is breaking. A growing share of searches now end without a click at all — the person asks, the AI answers, and they never visit a website. They just see the recommendation and act on it. If your business isn't in that answer, you weren't in the running, and you'll never see it in your analytics because there was no click to measure.

So the question isn't "am I ranking?" anymore. It's "when an AI answers the question my customer is actually asking, does it know I exist?"

## Where they overlap (and where they don't)

The good news: a lot of solid SEO work helps your AEO too. Clean site structure, fast load times, content that actually answers questions — AI engines like all of that for the same reasons Google does.

But some things only matter for one or the other:

- **Backlinks and keyword density** are classic SEO levers. They help you rank. They do much less for whether an AI cites you.
- **Schema markup** — the hidden, machine-readable labels that tell engines what your business is, where, and for whom — is now disproportionately an AEO lever. AI engines lean on it to understand and trust you. Most small-business sites have almost none.
- **Answer-shaped content** — a clear question with a direct, quotable answer right under it — is what lets an AI lift your text and attribute it to you. Generic marketing copy can rank fine and still be useless to an answer engine, because there's nothing clean to quote.
- **Your presence across the citation network** — Google Business Profile, maps, directories, reviews — feeds the local AI graph the engines pull from. This sits at the intersection, and it's where local businesses win or lose.

## So which one should you focus on?

Both — but the urgency has shifted, and here's why.

SEO is mature. Your competitors have been doing it for years, the playbook is well-known, and the gains are incremental. AEO is wide open. In most towns and most industries, almost no small businesses have done the work yet. That means the cost of being early is low and the payoff is high: when there are only a handful of AI-ready businesses in your category, the AI doesn't have many options to recommend.

That window won't stay open. The businesses that get cited now build up a track record the engines keep returning to. Catching up later means competing against an incumbent the AI already trusts.

So the honest answer: don't abandon SEO — it still drives real traffic, and the fundamentals carry over. But if you're choosing where the next dollar of attention goes, AEO is the one almost nobody around you is doing, and it's where your next customers are starting their search.

## What "doing AEO" actually involves

It's not a trick or a plugin. In practice it's three things working together:

1. **Content written so AI can extract clean answers** — real questions, direct answers, specifics over fluff.
2. **Full schema markup** — LocalBusiness, Service, FAQ, Review — so engines understand exactly what you do and can trust it.
3. **Presence across the local AI citation network** — a complete Google Business Profile, maps, and the directories the engines actually read.

None of it is exotic. Most of it is just doing the structural work that small-business sites skipped because, until recently, nothing was reading the structure.

## FAQ

**Is AEO replacing SEO?**
No. It's a second layer on top of it. Google's regular results aren't going away, and ranking still matters. But a real and growing share of searches now resolve inside an AI answer, and SEO alone doesn't put you there.

**Will doing AEO hurt my Google rankings?**
Usually the opposite. The structured data, faster site, and clearer content that AEO calls for tend to help classic rankings too. The two reinforce each other more than they conflict.

**How do I know if my site is already AI-ready?**
Ask an AI engine a question a customer would ask and see if you come up. If you don't, that's your answer. (A free 3-minute AI Readiness Audit will score it for you and tell you the top three things to fix.)

**How long until AEO work shows up?**
It varies by engine — Perplexity tends to reflect changes within days, AI Overviews within a few weeks, and the big chat models more gradually as they update. It's not instant, which is exactly why starting before your competitors matters.
